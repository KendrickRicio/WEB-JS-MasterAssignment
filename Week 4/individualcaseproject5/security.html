Computer Properties.

What kind of properties of your computer can web apps access?

Here are six of the many properties a web application can access to learn more about your computer:


Properties
onLine Returns a boolean value indicating whether the browser currently has a network connection
platform returns the operating system currently being used by the client computer
userAgent returns a string stored in the HTTP user-agent request header containing information about
the browser, the platform name, and compatibility.
availWidth returns the width of the display screen sans operating system features such as the taskbar
colorDepth returns the display screen's depth if a color palette is in use.
returns the value of the pixelDepth property if a color palette is not in use
pixelDepth returns the display screen's color resolution in bits per pixel





Practices for using the web safely

Here are three different ideas you can practice to keep your computer and data safer online:

  1. Stay up to date with software and security updates.
    They prevent vulnerabilities and exploits used by hackers to gain unintended access to your information.
    This article from Kaspersky provides information on how to stay safe online, including information on why you should stay up-to-date with your software.
  2. Use strong and unique passwords.
    Strong passwords are almost entirely necessary to protect your online accounts.
    Unique passwords increase the surface area of your account security, drastically increasing the effort needed to gain access to your other accounts.
    Having only one unique password greatly reduces the unique points of attack to one to gain access to all your accounts.
    You can also consider getting a password manager.
    StaySafeOnline.org is a national cybersecurity and online safety organization providing tips and resources on how to stay safe online.
    It also provides information on how to generate safe passwords.
  3. Be careful what you click on and where you enter your information.
    Phishing attacks use disguised links or forms to trick you into entering your information into the wrong areas.
    The Google Safety Center provides information on how to stay safe online, giving information about online privacy, security, and safety.
